Uses/Benefits

The applications of artificial intelligence are endless. The technology can be used for lab simulations, natural language processing, and self-driving cars.

In the field of research, artificial intelligence has made it easier to simulate certain control environments and experiment. It has been used to make learning and studying come to life for research students in fields where it’s not as easy to visualize concepts.

Another great use of artificial intelligence comes with natural language processing (NLP). NLP regards the understanding of sentiment and conversational text from a voice using artificial intelligence. Examples of this include Apple’s Siri, Google’s Assistant, and many more. These all allow us to interact with our world in a better, more efficient way and bridge the gap between machine-human interaction even further.

Lastly, artificial intelligence is used greatly through self-driving cars. With the rise of self-driving cars in the 21st century, we see AI being used to not only detect cars and obstacles, but also maneuver through them in a non-intrusive manner. Starting primarily with Tesla’s initial Model X car, users had the ability to use the “self-pilot” mode which would take in a 360-view of the car as input and output commands to the car itself to either stay between the lines, make a lane change, or stop at stop-light.

Technology

Artificial intelligence is built for all sorts of different platforms, but usually requires some sort of intensive computing at its very core. Its subsets include both machine learning and deep learning.

Machine learning and deep learning work based on the ideas of linear algebra, where your goal is to calculate certain weights for machine learning factors that will offer you the least percent error in your prediction or output. For example, let’s say you want to predict the weather. The goal of the artificial intelligence is solely to calculate a weight based on a factor (e.g. yesterday’s temperature) and predict an output for the next day given all the data from the past used for training.

For specific cases, taking into account videos and images, the machine learning and artificial intelligence algorithms take on many dimensions and become complex in terms of visualization. In those cases, we purely trust that the linear algebra is correct and make decisions, which has yielded amazing results thus far in the field, still very young.

Concerns

AI can cause concerns for people in their everyday lives.

In many aspects, researchers have a primary goal of keep their artificial intelligence socially beneficial. We do not want to start a devastating arms race with the powers of AI any time soon. The reality of artificial intelligence becoming iteratively more intelligent than humans is truly possible and is a concern that researchers fiddle with every day working on even the smallest projects. We can only advocate for stricter control on artificial intelligence and clearer guidelines in the case that something does prove itself to be dangerous in the future.
